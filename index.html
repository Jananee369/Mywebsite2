Black Holes

Where gravity defeats even light itself

Artist's Impression of a Black Hole System

What is a Black Hole?

A black hole is a region of spacetime where gravity is so incredibly strong that nothing — not even light — can escape once it crosses the event horizon. They form when massive stars collapse under their own gravity at the end of their life cycle.

Event Horizon

The point of no return — cross it, and you're gone forever. Not even light escapes.

Time Dilation

Time slows down near a black hole. 1 hour near one = years on Earth!

Spaghettification

Tidal forces would stretch you into a strand of spaghetti — literally!

Extreme Gravity

Supermassive black holes can be billions of times the mass of our Sun.

40 Billion km — diameter of M87*, the first black hole ever photographed

Dark Matter & Dark Energy

95% of the universe is a complete mystery

Universe Composition
Dark Energy 68%
Dark Matter 27%
Normal Matter 5%

Dark Matter

It doesn't emit, absorb, or reflect light — yet it makes up 27% of the universe. We know it exists because of its gravitational effects on galaxies. Without it, galaxies would fly apart!

Dark Energy

An even more mysterious force making up 68% of the universe. It's driving the accelerating expansion of the cosmos — pushing galaxies apart at increasing speed.

Why So Mysterious?

Despite decades of research, scientists have never directly detected dark matter or dark energy. We only know of their existence through cosmic observations. The universe is hiding its biggest secrets!

Section 04

Cosmic Events

The universe's most violent & spectacular phenomena

⭐ Supernova

Stellar Explosion

When a massive star exhausts its fuel, it collapses and explodes with the power of billions of suns combined. They seed the universe with heavy elements — including the iron in your blood!

10 billion °C peak Outshines entire galaxies
💥 GRB

Gamma-Ray Bursts

The most energetic explosions ever observed. A single burst releases more energy in seconds than our Sun will produce in its entire 10-billion-year lifetime.

Lasts 0.1 – 1000 seconds Speed of light jets
🌀 Neutron Star

Pulsars & Magnetars

The crushed core of a dead star, packed into a ball just 20 km wide. A teaspoon of neutron star material would weigh a billion tonnes. Pulsars spin up to 700 times per second!

1 billion tons/teaspoon 700 rotations/second
🌊 Gravity Waves

Gravitational Waves

Ripples in the fabric of spacetime itself, caused by merging black holes or neutron stars. First detected in 2015, they pass through everything — including you — right now!

Travel at light speed Distort space by 1/1000th of a proton
✨ Quasar

Quasars

The brightest objects in the universe — powered by supermassive black holes consuming enormous amounts of matter. A single quasar outshines entire galaxies of 100 billion stars.

1000x a galaxy's brightness 13+ billion light-years away
🔥 Big Bang

Birth of the Universe

13.8 billion years ago, all of space, time, matter and energy exploded from an infinitely small point. In the first second, the universe was hotter than any star that has ever existed.

10^32 °C at birth 13.8 billion years old
